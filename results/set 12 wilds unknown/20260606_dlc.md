# Disney Lorcana Challenge Side Event Dortmund 2026

Side event during the Dortmund challenge event. I opted to play a competetive core constructed side event on Saturday to still use some time while being in Dortmund. I specifically did not wanted to play another round of infinity constructed as the day before was not such a great success.

Tournament was played over six rounds with taking the Mulan promo as entry card and for each win 100 tix for the price wall. There was some additional pricing based on the number of wins - with a promo card at four wins, a display at five wins and then with six wins the special playmat.

## Tournament Rounds

As we did not played too much core constructed I sticked with my 🔵🟣 Amethyst Sapphire blurple deck - that I played as well during the team liga events. I expected some hard matches especially against 🟢🔵 Emerald Sapphire and against the faster 🟡🟢 Amber Emerald decks.

### Round 1

Round 1 was a paring into Luke from the UK at table 700. He played a 🟡🟣 Amber Amethyst deck, which is a tough matchup for me. In the first game he had some challenges with too high cost cards is his initial hand and by this I could ran away with the game. Second game was the other way round and he could get the win. In the last game I had some good cards in my hand where a Guidebook was sniped out by Mowgli and then my second source of card draw a Dumbo was removed by Horseman Strikes. From there on I was in a very weak position out of which I could not come back anymore.

### Round 4

Round 4 paired me then into Stefan from Germany. He seems to have some connections with the Playing Ducks team and fielded their favorite deck with 🟢🔵 Emerald Sapphire control. That deck got with the new Milo Thatch a significant upgrade and is always a grindy game. Game one went down to him as I could only find a single Demona and a single blue Hades - way to much to establish a sound board control - not even to speak of getting it back after a Prince Philipp combo. Second game went into my favor as I had there the pieces to close the game down - despite missing ramp on turn two. Last game was a real grind - we took it to the extra turns - but there I was able to close it. I was lucky with my top deck to find a Let It Go, after he played the third Prince Philipp down. In the end my Scuttle saved the day as it could quest for victory.
